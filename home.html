The first stock exchange is over 400 years old. It was established in Amsterdam in 1602 by the Dutch East India Company to issue the first publicly traded stocks. The NYSE started under a tree. On May 17, 1792, 24 stockbrokers met under a buttonwood tree on Wall Street to sign the Buttonwood Agreement, establishing the rules for trading U.S. stocks. The first recorded market bubble involved tulips. Known as the "Tulip Mania" of 1637 in the Netherlands, the price of single tulip bulbs skyrocketed to more than 10 times a skilled worker's annual income before crashing dramatically.
